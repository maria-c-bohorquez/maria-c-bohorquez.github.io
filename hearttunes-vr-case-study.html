The Concept & Challenge

Traditional adolescent socioemotional training modules struggle with low user engagement and lack safe, immersive environments for intense emotional expression. Developed as a close, collaborative co-creation by a dedicated product dupla (Maria Bohorquez & Laura Montenegro), HeartTunes explores how music-based virtual reality gaming can serve as a powerful tool for introspective self-awareness and regulation. Built as a native Unity prototype for the Meta/Oculus Quest, this joint design initiative translates musical audio tracks and physical virtual drumming interactions into direct, tangible metaphors for real-world emotional states.

Cross-Functional Co-Design Methodology

To ensure the product met strict educational standards and resonated deeply with real-world users, our product dupla utilized rigorous design thinking methodologies in close partnership with Coschool Colombia in Cali—a leading organization in Latin American socioemotional learning.

We facilitated continuous collaborative feedback loops, design sprints, and qualitative interviews with frontline educators in Cali. This product discovery work ensured that the core virtual reality features directly mapped onto pre-existing classroom curriculum goals, successfully aligning hardware performance expectations with the physical, operational realities of school environments.

Technical Ownership & Interaction Design

Operating as the technical product lead within the pairing, I steered cross-functional alignment and optimized system logic within the Unity engine to bring our product requirement documents (PRD) to life:

  • Product Discovery & Requirements Ownership: Orchestrated end-to-end user journey maps, defined technical feature dependencies, and managed rigorous backlog prioritization matrices to guide the prototype from abstract concept to deployable asset.
  • Hands-on QA & Validation Loops: Oversaw strict technical quality assurance pipelines within the spatial interface. I calibrated real-time velocity thresholds to ensure high-tempo virtual drumming inputs registered with absolute tracking accuracy and zero runtime latency, mitigating user immersion breakage.
  • Audio Engineering Strategy: Coordinated hand-in-hand with professional teachers and sound designers to construct specialized rhythm tracks. These adaptive tracks were engineered using culturally relevant rhythms and precise, structural silences designed to anchor, guide, and regulate users through intense emotional game sessions.

Prestige Outcomes & Recognition

The architecture, methodologies, and interactive user paradigms developed throughout this case study achieved critical industry validation:

  • Academic Placement: Successfully delivered and defended as a Major Research Project at Toronto Metropolitan University (formerly Ryerson University) in partial fulfillment of the degree of Master of Digital Media.
  • Global Peer Review Selection: Awarded a formal international industry invitation to present our interactive UX paradigms, research methodologies, and spatial analytics results at the prestigious IEEE GEM 2022 (Games, Entertainment, and Media) conference.
